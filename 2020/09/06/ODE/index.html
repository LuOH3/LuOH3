Thm. Poincare lemma

if $\Omega\subset R^2$ is open and simply connected, $P,Q\in C^1(\Omega,R)$, then $P(x,y)dx+Q(x,y)dy$ is exact iff $\partial_xQ=\partial_yP$

Proof. $\Rightarrow$ trivial

$\Leftarrow$ fix $(x_0,y_0)\in\Omega,\forall (x,y)\in\Omega$, we have $\gamma$ connects the two points

we define $\omega:=Pdx+Qdy$, and $u(x,y):=\int_\gamma \omega$

$u$ is well defined because by Green’s thm, the value of the integral is independent of the choice of $\gamma$

as $\Omega$ is open, $\exists \epsilon>0, B_\epsilon(x,y)\subset \Omega$, define $(x_1,y_1):=\partial B_\epsilon(x,y)\cap\gamma$, then $u(x,y)=\int_{(x_0,y_0)}^{(x_1,y_1)}\omega+\int_{(x_1,y_1)}^{(x,y)}\omega=\int_{(x_0,y_0)}^{(x_1,y_1)}\omega+\int_{x_1}^xP(t,y_1)dt+\int_{y_1}^yQ(x,t)dt$

$\partial_xu=P(x,y_1)+\int_{y_1}^y\partial_xQ(x,t)dt=P(x,y_1)+\int_{y_1}^y\partial_yP(x,t)dt=P(x,y),\partial_yu=Q(x,y)$

thus, $du=Pdx+Qdy=\omega,\omega$ is exact
